
### About me!

I am a PhD Candidate in the Department of Psychology at Yale University. 
As a member of the Turk-Browne Lab, I study early life memory development using awake infant fMRI.
My research focuses primarily on the functional development of the hippocampus and its involvement 
in episodic and relational memory processes during the first two years of life.

Before coming to Yale, I earned a Bachelor's in Neuroscience from Hamilton College (2020), 
and I spent two years as a research assistant in David E. Warren’s lab at the University of Nebraska Medical Center.
